J.R.R. Tolkien
1892 – 1973
Born in South Africa and orphaned by twelve, Tolkien was raised in the English countryside near Birmingham, where he fell in love with language, ancient myth, and the beauty of trees. He served as a signals officer with the Lancashire Fusiliers during World War I and survived the Battle of the Somme, where he lost two of his closest friends. After the war he became a professor of Anglo-Saxon at Oxford and spent decades building Middle-earth: an entire mythology drawn from Norse and Celtic legend, philology, and the quiet English landscape. The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings redefined fantasy literature. His works have never gone out of print.
