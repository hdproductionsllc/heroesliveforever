# HEROES LIVE FOREVER — Marketing & Growth Plan

## Executive Summary

Heroes Live Forever sells emotion, not frames. Every marketing decision flows from one insight: **people have an almost primal need to honor the people who shaped them.** A father who served. A coach who believed in them. The athlete who made them fall in love with the game. This need is deep, underserved, and highly shareable.

The product is inherently viral because:
- It's deeply personal and visual (social-native content)
- It triggers pride and identity (people WANT to be seen honoring their heroes)
- It's a gift product (every sale creates a new audience)
- It taps into tribal communities (teams, branches, alumni, fandoms)

This plan builds a growth engine that compounds — where every customer becomes a marketer, every unboxing becomes content, and every community we enter opens three more.

## The Core Viral Loops

### Loop 1: The Reveal Moment

This is the engine. Every Heroes Live Forever piece has a **reveal moment** — the first time someone sees it hung on the wall or unwrapped from the box. That moment is deeply emotional and highly filmable.

```
Customer receives piece
        ↓
Emotional reveal moment (unboxing, hanging, gifting)
        ↓
Customer photographs/films it → posts to social media
        ↓
Friends/family see it → "Where did you get that?"
        ↓
Tagged brand / link in comments / DM inquiry
        ↓
New customer orders their own hero
        ↓
REPEAT
```

**How we engineer this:**
- Include a printed card in every box: "Share your hero. Tag @heroesliveforever for a chance to be featured."
- QR code on the back of every frame linking to "Share Your Hero" page
- The packaging itself should be unboxing-worthy: black tissue paper, wax seal, hand-signed certificate of authenticity
- Ship with a small "Heroes Live Forever" branded easel stand for the 8x10 size so people display it immediately

**Conversion trigger:** Social proof. When someone sees a real person's genuine emotional reaction to receiving a tribute of their father's military service, that's not an ad. That's a conversion machine.

### Loop 2: The Gift Loop

This is the most powerful loop because it has a built-in multiplier.

```
Person A buys piece as gift for Person B
        ↓
Person B receives it → deeply moved
        ↓
Person B shows it to Persons C, D, E (family, friends, visitors)
        ↓
Persons C, D, E now want one for THEIR hero
        ↓
Some buy as gifts for OTHERS
        ↓
Each gift creates 2-5 new exposures
        ↓
COMPOUND
```

**How we engineer this:**
- Every shipment includes 2-3 branded "gift cards" with a discount code: "Know someone who deserves a hero tribute? 15% off their first piece."
- The discount code is unique and trackable — we know which customers drive referrals
- After purchase, automated email: "Who else in your life has a hero worth honoring? Father's Day is X days away."
- Gift wrapping option with handwritten note card (premium add-on that increases perceived value and shareability)

### Loop 3: The Tribal Loop

People identify with groups — military branches, sports teams, universities, nationalities. When one member of a tribe gets a piece, others follow.

```
One Marines veteran hangs a "Dan Daly" tribute in the VFW hall
        ↓
Other Marines see it → "I need one of Chesty Puller"
        ↓
Word spreads through the post/chapter
        ↓
Group orders or individual orders spike from that zip code
        ↓
We approach the VFW/Legion post about a display partnership
        ↓
Permanent display in common area → ongoing exposure
        ↓
EXPAND to next post/chapter
```

**How we engineer this:**
- Identify and seed pieces into community hubs (VFW halls, team bars, alumni offices, barbershops, coaches' offices)
- Offer "Community Display" pricing: free or deeply discounted piece for permanent public display in exchange for a small branded plaque and QR code
- Launch with the tightest tribal communities first: Marine Corps (legendary esprit de corps), specific NFL fanbases (Steelers, Cowboys, Packers — multigenerational loyalty), and university alumni networks

### Loop 4: The Content Loop

Every hero has a story. Every story is content. Every piece of content drives discovery.

```
We create a piece for a new hero
        ↓
We produce a short-form video telling their story (60-90 seconds)
        ↓
Post across TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
        ↓
History/sports/military community engages
        ↓
Comments section becomes discovery engine:
"Do you have one for [other hero]?"
"Can you make one for my grandfather?"
        ↓
Each video is a product ad disguised as storytelling
        ↓
New orders → new heroes → new stories → new videos
        ↓
FLYWHEEL
```

**How we engineer this:**
- Produce 3-5 hero story videos per week (can be done with stock footage, photos, Ken Burns effect, voiceover)
- Format: dramatic hook (3 seconds) → hero's story (45-60 seconds) → reveal of the finished piece (15 seconds) → CTA
- Every video ends with the piece slowly rotating or being hung, with the brand wordmark
- Encourage UGC: "Tell us your hero's story in the comments. The most powerful story each month gets a free custom piece."

### Tier 2: Paid Acquisition

#### Facebook/Instagram Ads

**Audience targeting:**
- Military: Veterans, active duty, military spouses, VFW members, military history enthusiasts
- Sports: Team-specific fan pages, sports memorabilia buyers, fantasy sports players, season ticket holders
- Historical: History channel viewers, museum members, book club members, educators
- Gift buyers: Father's Day shoppers, graduation gift searchers, sympathy/memorial gift searchers

**Ad creative strategy:**
- Lead with emotion, not product specs
- Video ads outperform static for this product (the reveal moment IS the ad)
- Best performing format: customer reaction video with text overlay telling the hero's story
- A/B test: hero story focus vs. product/craftsmanship focus vs. gift angle

**Campaign structure:**
1. **Top of funnel (TOF):** Hero story videos. No hard sell. Goal: engagement, saves, shares, profile visits. Budget: 50% of ad spend.
2. **Middle of funnel (MOF):** Retarget video viewers with product-focused content. Show the piece, the detail, the quality. Include customer testimonials. Budget: 30%.
3. **Bottom of funnel (BOF):** Retarget website visitors and cart abandoners. Urgency and social proof. "Your hero deserves a place on the wall." Budget: 20%.

**Seasonal surges (increase spend 3x):**
- Father's Day (4 weeks before)
- Veterans Day / Memorial Day (2 weeks before)
- Christmas (6 weeks before)
- Graduation season (April-May)

#### Licensing Considerations

**Public domain heroes (no licensing needed):**
- Any historical figure with public domain imagery (pre-1929 photos, or government works)
- Military figures (most official military photos are government works = public domain)
- Classical musicians, authors, historical leaders (with PD portraits)

**Licensed heroes (requires agreements):**
- Active or recently retired athletes (need likeness rights or league licensing)
- Living public figures
- Team logos and branded imagery

**Strategy:** Launch the Signature Collection entirely with public domain subjects. Prove the model. Then approach licensing for modern athletes and figures once you have sales data and brand credibility.

## The "Who's Your Hero?" Campaign

This is the tentpole brand campaign that drives everything.

### Concept
Ask one simple question across every channel: **"Who's your hero?"**

This question is:
- Universally relatable (everyone has an answer)
- Deeply personal (the answer reveals something about you)
- Inherently shareable (people love talking about who inspires them)
- A natural product funnel ("Now imagine them on your wall")

### Execution

**Phase 1: Seed (Weeks 1-4)**
- Launch "Who's Your Hero?" as a social media series
- David films himself answering the question, explaining his hero and why
- Post across all platforms with the hashtag #WhoIsYourHero
- Encourage followers to duet/stitch/reply with their own answers
- No product push yet — just the question and genuine stories

**Phase 2: Connect (Weeks 5-8)**
- Start featuring the best audience responses
- For each featured response, reveal that we created a Heroes Live Forever piece of their hero
- Ship the piece to the person who shared the story (free, as a surprise)
- Film their reaction when they receive it
- NOW the audience connects the emotional story to the physical product

**Phase 3: Scale (Weeks 9+)**
- "Who's Your Hero?" becomes a monthly contest
- Submit your hero's story for a chance to win a free custom piece
- User submissions become a content goldmine (with permission)
- The hashtag becomes the brand's social proof engine
- Partner with podcasts and YouTube channels to run "Who's Your Hero?" segments

### Projected Viral Mechanics
- Each "Who's Your Hero?" response post averages 3-10x higher engagement than product posts (because it's about THEM, not us)
- Each giveaway reveal video has potential for 50K-500K views on TikTok (emotional reveals are the platform's bread and butter)
- Each winner becomes a lifetime brand advocate (they received something priceless and personal)

## Referral Program

### Structure: "Honor a Friend"

- Every customer gets a unique referral link after purchase
- When someone uses their link to buy, the referrer gets $20 store credit
- The new customer gets 10% off their first order
- Track and display on customer dashboard: "You've helped honor X heroes"

### Program Mechanics
- Referral link included in: order confirmation email, shipping confirmation, package insert card, customer account page
- Gamification tier: refer 3 friends = "Hero Advocate" status (15% lifetime discount). Refer 10 = "Hero Ambassador" (20% + early access to new pieces)
- Top referrers get featured on social media and website

### Why This Works for This Product
- People who buy Heroes Live Forever pieces are PROUD of them
- They naturally tell friends and family about it
- The referral program simply formalizes what's already happening
- The emotional nature of the product makes referrals feel like sharing something meaningful, not hawking a discount code

## Competitive Moat

Why Heroes Live Forever wins long-term:

1. **Design system, not templates.** Every piece is category-sensitive. Competitors sell generic frames with printed photos. We sell design-intelligent tributes where the colors, materials, and typography respond to the subject.

2. **3D-printed custom mats.** This is a real manufacturing differentiator. Traditional mat cutting is limited. 3D printing allows complex multi-window layouts that would be prohibitively expensive to hand-cut at scale.

3. **Content flywheel.** Every new hero is a new piece of content. The more heroes we honor, the more stories we tell, the more audience we build, the more heroes people request. The product IS the content.

4. **Community ownership.** "Who's Your Hero?" turns customers into co-creators. They're not just buying a product — they're participating in a movement to honor the people who matter.

5. **Emotional switching cost.** Once someone has a Heroes Live Forever piece on their wall, they don't switch to a competitor for their next one. They come back to us because the brand is now part of how they honor the people they love.
